# AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (AWS CAF)

## Introduction

The **AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (AWS CAF)** provides guidance and best practices to help organizations build a comprehensive approach to cloud computing across the organization. It helps organizations accelerate successful cloud adoption throughout the IT lifecycle. The AWS CAF is organized into **perspectives**, which consist of sets of capabilities that span people, processes, and technology.

- **Explore the core perspectives of the AWS CAF**: Learn about the six core perspectives and their respective capabilities.

## AWS CAF Defined

The AWS CAF is designed to help organizations:
- **Build a comprehensive approach** to cloud computing.
- **Accelerate successful cloud adoption** by aligning people, processes, and technology.
- **Break down the complex process** of cloud adoption into manageable pieces called **perspectives**.

Each organization’s cloud adoption journey is unique, but the AWS CAF provides a structured approach to ensure that **business and technology leaders** understand the current state, target state, and the transition needed to achieve cloud adoption goals.

---

## Core Perspectives of the AWS CAF

The AWS CAF organizes guidance into six core perspectives, each focusing on either **business capabilities** or **technical capabilities**.

### 1. **Business Perspective**
   - **Capabilities**:
     - IT finance
     - IT strategy
     - Benefits realization
     - Business risk management
   - **Stakeholders**: Business managers, finance managers, budget owners, and strategy stakeholders.
   - **Focus**: Aligning IT with business needs and ensuring IT investments deliver measurable business results.
   - **Example**: A company might use the Business perspective to justify the ROI of migrating to the cloud by showing how it reduces operational costs and improves scalability.

### 2. **People Perspective**
   - **Capabilities**:
     - Resource management
     - Incentive management
     - Career management
     - Training management
     - Organizational change management
   - **Stakeholders**: Human resources, staffing, and people managers.
   - **Focus**: Prioritizing training, staffing, and organizational changes to build an agile organization.
   - **Example**: An organization might use the People perspective to identify skill gaps in their IT team and implement a training program for cloud technologies.

### 3. **Governance Perspective**
   - **Capabilities**:
     - Portfolio management
     - Program and project management
     - Business performance measurement
     - License management
   - **Stakeholders**: CIO, program managers, enterprise architects, business analysts, and portfolio managers.
   - **Focus**: Aligning IT strategy with business strategy to maximize the value of IT investments and minimize risks.
   - **Example**: A company might use the Governance perspective to ensure that cloud adoption projects are aligned with the overall business strategy and are delivered on time and within budget.

### 4. **Platform Perspective**
   - **Capabilities**:
     - Compute provisioning
     - Network provisioning
     - Storage provisioning
     - Database provisioning
     - Systems and solution architecture
     - Application development
   - **Stakeholders**: CTO, IT managers, and solutions architects.
   - **Focus**: Understanding and communicating the architecture of the target state environment.
   - **Example**: A company might use the Platform perspective to design a cloud architecture that supports scalability and high availability for their applications.

### 5. **Security Perspective**
   - **Capabilities**:
     - Identity and access management
     - Detective control
     - Infrastructure security
     - Data protection
     - Incident response
   - **Stakeholders**: CISO, IT security managers, and IT security analysts.
   - **Focus**: Ensuring the organization meets its security objectives for visibility, auditability, control, and agility.
   - **Example**: A company might use the Security perspective to implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) and encryption for data at rest and in transit.

### 6. **Operations Perspective**
   - **Capabilities**:
     - Service monitoring
     - Application performance monitoring
     - Resource inventory management
     - Release management or change management
     - Reporting and analytics
     - Business continuity or disaster recovery (DR)
     - IT service catalog
   - **Stakeholders**: IT operations managers and IT support managers.
   - **Focus**: Defining how day-to-day, quarter-to-quarter, and year-to-year business operations will be conducted.
   - **Example**: A company might use the Operations perspective to implement automated monitoring and alerting for their cloud infrastructure to ensure high availability.

## Key Ideas

- The AWS CAF helps organizations develop efficient and effective plans for their cloud adoption journey.
- The AWS CAF breaks down the complex process of planning a move to the cloud into manageable pieces called **perspectives**.
- Perspectives consist of sets of business or technology capabilities that are the responsibility of key stakeholders.
- The AWS CAF provides a structure for business and IT teams to work together to adopt the cloud successfully.

---

## Additional Notes

- **Business Perspective**: This perspective ensures that cloud adoption aligns with the overall business strategy. For example, a retail company might use this perspective to ensure that their cloud infrastructure supports seasonal traffic spikes during holiday sales.
  
- **People Perspective**: This perspective focuses on the human element of cloud adoption. For instance, a company might need to retrain its IT staff to manage cloud infrastructure or hire new talent with cloud expertise.

- **Governance Perspective**: This perspective ensures that cloud adoption is managed effectively. For example, a company might use governance to track the progress of multiple cloud projects and ensure they are aligned with business goals.

- **Platform Perspective**: This perspective is crucial for designing the technical architecture of the cloud environment. For example, a company might use this perspective to decide whether to use a multi-cloud strategy or stick to a single cloud provider.

- **Security Perspective**: This perspective ensures that the cloud environment is secure. For example, a company might use this perspective to implement encryption and access controls to protect sensitive data.

- **Operations Perspective**: This perspective focuses on the day-to-day management of cloud resources. For example, a company might use this perspective to implement automated backups and disaster recovery plans.
